# Thinking Hat Sequences

Use the lightest adequate structure. Not every situation needs a full multi-hat journey: sometimes a rapid pulse check or a single direct hat pass is enough.

For non-sequence patterns such as Socratic interrogation, decision comparison, pre-mortems, and rapid pulse checks, see `assets/mode-catalog.md`.

Eight pre-built hat sequences from De Bono's methodology. The Blue Hat (Facilitator) selects the best sequence based on the user's goal and may adapt the order mid-session if needed.

---

## Quick Reference

| # | Sequence | Flow | Best For |
|---|----------|------|----------|
| 1 | Initial Ideas | B→W→G→B | Exploring a new topic |
| 2 | Choosing Between Alternatives | B→W→(G)→Y→K→R→B | Evaluating options, making a decision |
| 3 | Identifying Solutions | B→W→K→G→B | Solving a known problem |
| 4 | Quick Feedback | B→K→G→B | Rapid critique + improvement |
| 5 | Strategic Planning | B→Y→K→W→B→G→B | Long-range plans, major decisions |
| 6 | Process Improvement | B→W→W→Y→K→G→R→B | Improving existing workflows |
| 7 | Problem Solving | B→W→G→R→Y→K→G→B | Complex problems needing full exploration |
| 8 | Performance Review | B→R→W→Y→K→G→B | Retrospectives, evaluating past work |

**Key:** B=Blue, W=White, R=Red, Y=Yellow, K=Black, G=Green. Parentheses = optional step.

---

## Sequence 1: Initial Ideas

**Use when:** Exploring a new topic or generating first thoughts about an unfamiliar area.

**Trigger phrases:** "I have a new idea," "Let's brainstorm," "What do we think about...," "I want to explore..."

**Flow:** Blue → White → Green → Blue

| Step | Hat | Duration | What Happens |
|------|-----|----------|--------------|
| 1 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | State the topic and set the goal: "We want to generate initial ideas about X." |
| 2 | ⚪ White | 5 min | Map what is known about the topic. Identify data, facts, and information gaps. |
| 3 | 🟢 Green | 5 min | Generate ideas freely based on the facts. Go wide—no judgment. Use provocations and lateral thinking. |
| 4 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Summarize: facts gathered, ideas generated, and what to explore next. |

**Total time:** ~14 minutes

**What to expect:** A broad set of raw ideas grounded in what's actually known. This is a divergent sequence—it opens possibilities rather than narrowing them.

---

## Sequence 2: Choosing Between Alternatives

**Use when:** You have 2+ options and need to evaluate and select one.

**Trigger phrases:** "Should we go with A or B?," "Which option is best?," "Help me decide between...," "I'm torn between..."

**Flow:** Blue → White → (Green) → Yellow → Black → Red → Blue

| Step | Hat | Duration | What Happens |
|------|-----|----------|--------------|
| 1 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Name the options. Set the goal: evaluate and choose. |
| 2 | ⚪ White | 3 min | Gather facts about each option. What data supports or distinguishes them? |
| 3 | 🟢 Green | 3 min | *(Optional)* Are there options we haven't considered? Any creative combinations? |
| 4 | 🟡 Yellow | 3 min | What are the benefits of each option? Why might each work? |
| 5 | ⚫ Black | 3 min | What are the risks of each option? Why might each fail? |
| 6 | 🔴 Red | 1 min | Gut check: which option feels right? Quick pulse, no explanation needed. |
| 7 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Synthesize: pros, cons, gut feel. State the decision or next step. |

**Total time:** ~14-17 minutes

**What to expect:** A structured evaluation where benefits, risks, and intuition each get dedicated space. The Red Hat at the end often breaks ties the logical analysis can't.

## Sequence 3: Identifying Solutions

**Use when:** A known problem needs workable solutions.

**Trigger phrases:** "How do we fix...," "What's the solution to...," "We have a problem with...," "I need to solve..."

**Flow:** Blue → White → Black → Green → Blue

| Step | Hat | Duration | What Happens |
|------|-----|----------|--------------|
| 1 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Define the problem clearly. Set the goal: find workable solutions. |
| 2 | ⚪ White | 4 min | Map what is known about the problem. Root causes, data, history. |
| 3 | ⚫ Black | 3 min | What constraints limit solutions? What has been tried and failed? Why? |
| 4 | 🟢 Green | 5 min | Given the constraints, generate creative solutions. Use provocations to break past obvious answers. |
| 5 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Summarize solutions found. Identify which to evaluate further. |

**Total time:** ~16 minutes

**What to expect:** Solutions that are informed by real constraints. The Black-before-Green order is intentional: knowing what won't work channels creativity toward what might.

---

## Sequence 4: Quick Feedback

**Use when:** Rapid assessment of an idea or proposal. The lightest sequence.

**Trigger phrases:** "What do you think of this?," "Quick gut check on...," "Poke holes in this," "Give me feedback on..."

**Flow:** Blue → Black → Green → Blue

| Step | Hat | Duration | What Happens |
|------|-----|----------|--------------|
| 1 | 🔵 Blue | 1 min | Frame the proposal. Goal: identify problems and suggest fixes. |
| 2 | ⚫ Black | 2 min | What's wrong with this? What could go wrong? Be specific. |
| 3 | 🟢 Green | 3 min | How do we fix the problems identified? What alternatives exist? |
| 4 | 🔵 Blue | 1 min | Summary: issues found, solutions proposed, verdict. |

**Total time:** ~7 minutes

**What to expect:** A fast, focused cycle. Critique is immediately followed by construction—you don't just tear down, you rebuild.

## Sequence 5: Strategic Planning

**Use when:** Long-range planning, major investments, or high-stakes decisions.

**Trigger phrases:** "Let's plan our strategy for...," "Should we invest in...," "What's our 5-year approach to...," "Help me think through this big decision"

**Flow:** Blue → Yellow → Black → White → Blue → Green → Blue

| Step | Hat | Duration | What Happens |
|------|-----|----------|--------------|
| 1 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Frame the strategic question. Set scope and time horizon. |
| 2 | 🟡 Yellow | 3 min | What's the opportunity? Why is this worth pursuing? What's the upside? |
| 3 | ⚫ Black | 3 min | What could derail this? Strategic risks, competitive threats, resource gaps. |
| 4 | ⚪ White | 4 min | What data do we need to validate the opportunity and mitigate the risks? |
| 5 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Mid-session check: where are we? Adjust sequence if needed. |
| 6 | 🟢 Green | 5 min | Given opportunities, risks, and data gaps—what creative strategies emerge? |
| 7 | 🔵 Blue | 3 min | Final synthesis: strategy recommendation with caveats and next actions. |

**Total time:** ~22 minutes

**What to expect:** Yellow-first is intentional—start with why it matters before stress-testing. The mid-session Blue check lets the facilitator adapt if the discussion has shifted the question.

---

## Sequence 6: Process Improvement

**Use when:** Improving an existing process, workflow, or system.

**Trigger phrases:** "How do we improve...," "This process is broken," "Let's do a process review of...," "What's working and what isn't?"

**Flow:** Blue → White → White → Yellow → Black → Green → Red → Blue

| Step | Hat | Duration | What Happens |
|------|-----|----------|--------------|
| 1 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Name the process to improve. Goal: identify what works, what doesn't, and how to fix it. |
| 2 | ⚪ White | 3 min | Facts about the current process: metrics, throughput, frequency, costs. |
| 3 | ⚪ White | 3 min | Stakeholder perspectives: what would customers, partners, or new hires say about this process? |
| 4 | 🟡 Yellow | 2 min | What's working well? What should we preserve? |
| 5 | ⚫ Black | 3 min | What's broken? Pain points, failure modes, bottlenecks. |
| 6 | 🟢 Green | 4 min | How do we fix what's broken while preserving what works? |
| 7 | 🔴 Red | 1 min | How do people feel about the proposed changes? Quick emotional pulse. |
| 8 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Synthesis: changes to make, what to preserve, emotional temperature of the team. |

**Total time:** ~20 minutes

**What to expect:** The double White Hat pass (internal data + external perspectives) prevents tunnel vision. The Red Hat near the end catches change resistance early.

## Sequence 7: Problem Solving

**Use when:** Complex problem requiring full, deep exploration. The most thorough sequence.

**Trigger phrases:** "This is a hard problem," "We've been stuck on...," "I need to think deeply about...," "Full analysis of..."

**Flow:** Blue → White → Green → Red → Yellow → Black → Green → Blue

| Step | Hat | Duration | What Happens |
|------|-----|----------|--------------|
| 1 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Define the problem. Goal: generate and evaluate solutions comprehensively. |
| 2 | ⚪ White | 4 min | Full information landscape. What do we know? What don't we? What's uncertain? |
| 3 | 🟢 Green | 4 min | First creative pass: generate possible solutions freely. Quantity over quality. |
| 4 | 🔴 Red | 1 min | Gut reactions to the ideas so far. Which feel promising? Which feel wrong? |
| 5 | 🟡 Yellow | 3 min | Take the promising ideas: why might each work? Build the case. |
| 6 | ⚫ Black | 3 min | Stress test: what could go wrong with each promising idea? |
| 7 | 🟢 Green | 3 min | Second creative pass: knowing the risks, improve the solutions. Combine, adapt, invent. |
| 8 | 🔵 Blue | 3 min | Final synthesis: recommended solution with risk mitigation plan and next steps. |

**Total time:** ~23 minutes

**What to expect:** The two Green Hat passes are the signature move—first to generate raw ideas, second to refine them after stress-testing. Red Hat between generation and evaluation uses intuition as a filter.

---

## Sequence 8: Performance Review

**Use when:** Evaluating past performance, running retrospectives, reviewing how something went.

**Trigger phrases:** "How did that go?," "Let's do a retro on...," "Review our performance on...," "What did we learn from...?"

**Flow:** Blue → Red → White → Yellow → Black → Green → Blue

| Step | Hat | Duration | What Happens |
|------|-----|----------|--------------|
| 1 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Name what's being reviewed. Goal: honest assessment and improvement plan. |
| 2 | 🔴 Red | 1 min | First impressions: how do we FEEL about how it went? Raw emotional data before analysis. |
| 3 | ⚪ White | 4 min | What actually happened? Numbers, timelines, deliverables—facts only. |
| 4 | 🟡 Yellow | 3 min | What went well? What should we do MORE of? |
| 5 | ⚫ Black | 3 min | What went wrong? What should we do LESS of or stop doing? |
| 6 | 🟢 Green | 4 min | How do we improve for next time? New approaches, experiments, changes. |
| 7 | 🔵 Blue | 2 min | Synthesis: wins to celebrate, lessons learned, concrete action items for improvement. |

**Total time:** ~19 minutes

**What to expect:** Red Hat first is deliberate—capture emotional truth before facts reshape the narrative. The "feelings about how it went" are often the most honest data point.

## Choosing the Right Sequence

The Blue Hat should match the sequence to the user's situation:

| User's Situation | Best Sequence |
|---|---|
| "I have a vague idea I want to explore" | 1 — Initial Ideas |
| "I need to pick between options" | 2 — Choosing Between Alternatives |
| "I know the problem, need a fix" | 3 — Identifying Solutions |
| "Quick thoughts on this?" | 4 — Quick Feedback |
| "Big decision, high stakes" | 5 — Strategic Planning |
| "This workflow needs improvement" | 6 — Process Improvement |
| "Complex problem, need deep thinking" | 7 — Problem Solving |
| "How did X go? What did we learn?" | 8 — Performance Review |

If the user's goal doesn't fit neatly into one sequence, the Blue Hat should:
1. Pick the closest match
2. Explain the choice
3. Offer to customize by adding, removing, or reordering hats

Custom sequences are fine—the canonical 8 are starting points, not constraints.
