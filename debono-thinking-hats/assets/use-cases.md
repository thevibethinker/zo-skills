# Thinking Hats — Use Cases

## Product Strategy
- Evaluate a new feature launch
- Compare pricing models
- Run a pre-mortem on a product bet
- Use Socratic White/Black to interrogate assumptions before roadmap commitment

## Team / Management
- Run a retro with Red → White → Yellow → Black → Green
- Compare hiring candidates with White, Yellow, Black, then Red pulse
- Use Blue to keep emotionally loaded conversations structured

## Writing / Communication
- Use White to gather source material
- Use Yellow to surface strongest benefit framing
- Use Black to stress-test credibility or objections
- Use Green to generate new angles or analogies

## Personal Decisions
- Career choice: White facts, Red gut signal, Yellow upside, Black risk, Blue synthesis
- Relationship or partnership question: Red + White + Black balance
- Lifestyle changes: rapid pulse check first, full sequence only if needed

## Research / Analysis
- White for evidence map
- Black for fragility and missing support
- Green for alternative hypotheses
- Socratic mode when the goal is better questions rather than a fast conclusion

## Creative Work
- Green for divergence
- Yellow for promising directions
- Black for constraints that can sharpen the concept
- Blue to prevent creative collapse into premature criticism

## Operational Problems
- White for current-state map
- Black for failure points
- Green for redesign options
- Yellow for practical gains
- Blue to convert into next actions

## Rule of Thumb
If the problem is blurry, start with Blue or White.
If the problem is emotionally loaded, include Red early.
If the problem is promising but risky, balance Yellow and Black.
If the problem feels stuck, bring in Green.
If the whole thing is chaotic, return to Blue.
